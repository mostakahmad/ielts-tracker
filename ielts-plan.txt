IELTS Band 7+ 45-Day Self-Study Plan

Exam: Academic IELTS
Current level: Beginner
Daily time: 4 hours — Morning 2h + Evening/Night 2h
Target: Listening 7+, Reading 7+, Writing 7+, Speaking 7+

Band 7 from beginner in 45 days is very ambitious, so your target should be:
Day 1–15: Build foundation
Day 16–30: Learn IELTS techniques + question types
Day 31–45: Full mock tests + correction + Band 7 polishing

IELTS has four sections: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking. Academic IELTS has different Reading and Writing tasks from General Training, while Listening and Speaking are the same for both formats.

4. Skill-Wise Plan

18. Strategy for Improving from Band 6 to Band 7+

Once you reach around Band 6, your problem will not be “knowledge only.” It will be accuracy, consistency, and control.

Band 6 to Band 7 Upgrade Plan
Area	Band 6 Problem	Band 7 Fix
Listening	Loses marks in Sections 3/4	Practice distractors + transcript analysis
Reading	Runs out of time	Strict passage timing
Writing	Ideas are simple or unclear	Plan before writing
Writing	Grammar errors reduce score	Use fewer but more accurate complex sentences
Speaking	Hesitation and repetition	Record daily and answer with structure
Vocabulary	Basic words repeated	Use topic collocations
Grammar	Too many small errors	Maintain correction notebook
Band 7 Writing Upgrade Formula

Before writing any essay, spend 5 minutes planning:

Step	Question
1	What is the exact question type?
2	What is my clear position?
3	What are my two main ideas?
4	What example can I use?
5	What vocabulary fits this topic?
Band 7 Speaking Upgrade Formula

For every answer, use:

Answer → Reason → Example → Extra detail

Example:
“I prefer studying in the morning because my concentration is much better at that time. For example, when I study vocabulary after breakfast, I can remember words more easily than at night.”

19. Best Practical Daily Rule

For the next 45 days, follow this rule:

Never only practice. Always practice + correct.

Bad method:
“I solved one listening test.”

Good method:
“I solved one listening test, checked every wrong answer, listened again with transcript, wrote the reason for each mistake, and repeated the weak section.”

That correction habit is what moves you toward Band 7+.
